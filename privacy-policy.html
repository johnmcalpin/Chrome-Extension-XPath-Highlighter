Privacy Policy for XPath Highlighter

Last Updated: December 22, 2024

Overview

XPath Highlighter is committed to protecting your privacy. This extension operates entirely locally in your browser and does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data.

Data Collection

We do not collect any data. Period.

XPath Highlighter:

  • Does not collect personal information
  • Does not track your browsing activity
  • Does not use cookies or analytics
  • Does not transmit data to external servers
  • Does not store data in the cloud

How the Extension Works

XPath Highlighter operates entirely within your browser:

  1. When you click an element on a webpage, the extension generates an XPath expression
  2. The XPath expression is displayed in the side panel
  3. All processing happens locally in your browser
  4. No data leaves your device

Permissions Explanation

The extension requests the following permissions:

activeTab

Allows the extension to interact with the currently active tab when you click the extension icon. This is necessary to detect element clicks and generate XPath expressions.

scripting

Allows the extension to inject the content script that detects your element selections on webpages.

sidePanel

Allows the extension to display the XPath results in a side panel for your convenience.

Access to all websites (<all_urls>)

This permission is required so the extension can work on any website you visit. The extension only activates when you explicitly use it and does not run in the background on websites you're not actively using it on.

Third-Party Services

This extension does not use any third-party services, analytics, or advertising networks.

Data Storage

The extension does not store any persistent data. All generated XPath expressions exist only temporarily in your browser's memory while the extension is in use.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
